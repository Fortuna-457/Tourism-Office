ANDALUCÍA

Just feel.

Since time immemorial, Andalucía has been known as a point of union, not only geographically but also culturally and religiously. As many peoples have left their mark on this wonderful land, it is not possible to express in words what Andalucía is.


Andalucía is sun and snow, sea and mountains, it is Christian and Muslim, it is both a way of living and a way of feeling. It has so many facets that it is impossible to know them all, so just feel and let Andalucía show you all of them.

WHAT TO DO...?

  • Beaches

    A coastline that is a true paradise of beaches, both for resting and for enjoying water sports. All this together with the friendliness, the culture, the tasty gastronomy and the good work that we find throughout Andalusia.

  • Castles

    Andalusia stands out for the abundance of castles that have survived to the present day, where a total of 237 vestiges between citadels, bastions-atalaya, walls and castles are gathered.

  • Desert

    Located in the southeast of Andalusia, it is the driest region in Europe and the only "authentic" desert in the Old Continent, with more than 3,000 hours of sunshine per year.

  • Natural Areas

    Andalusia has more than 310 protected natural areas, each of which shines with its own light. Places as emblematic as Doñana, Sierra de las Nieves or Sierra Nevada in Granada, declared "Biosphere Reserve".

  • Las Alpujarras

    A place eccentric and different from everything else, a legacy of the last Moors mixed with the blood of old Christians after exhausting battles that had been cornered in the history books.

  • Sun & Snow

    Its 115.6 Km. skiable in 120 marked trails, the quality of its snow, the exceptional treatment of its slopes and the complementary leisure offer make Sierra Nevada an international reference.

PROVINCES

  • Granada

    Where the Alhambra sighs in the dawn. Under the moon, your beauty is unrivaled. Hidden waters that cry, says a popular saying: "There is no greater sorrow than to be blind in Granada".

  • Cádiz

    From Cadiz, the sea, its people, its bay, its history and its desire to live. From here came many of the great artists of this wonderful land that is Andalusia.

  • Huelva

    Huelva, where the sun kisses the sea and the land is dressed in green. Your streets, witnesses of stories and legends, whisper to the ear of the wind the secrets of ancient sailors.

  • Córdoba

    Jewel of a thousand colors. Where the Guadalquivir sighs love. Your Mosque is a dream of alabaster. You are poetry, you are art, you are trace.

  • Sevilla

    The Guadalquivir dances. Your Giralda rises, in the sky throws. Your Alcazar, a dream of stone and flower. You are music, you are passion, you are color.

  • Almería

    The jewel of the southeast, where the sun caresses the sea with its warm embrace. Your beaches, of fine golden sand, are the scenery where the Mediterranean unfolds its majesty.

  • Jaén

    Olive trees spread like a green sea under the blue sky. Your castles, witnesses of ages past, stand proudly on the hills, telling stories of heroes and battles.

  • Málaga

    Where the sea bathes its beaches with love. Proud Alcazaba, canvas of sea and sun. Museums of art, melting pot of tradition. Night of stars, reflection of passion.

HISTORY

The history of Andalusia dates back to the Neolithic, being home to advanced civilizations such as the Tartessians, Phoenicians and Greeks. It was part of the Roman Empire and Al-Andalus.

In the 13th century, it became Castilian and then Spanish territory. In 1980, after a referendum, Andalusia became an autonomous community of Spain.

This has made Andalusian a privileged land where vestiges of different civilizations and eras coexist. Offering the visitor an unique journey to different parts of history in the same place.

WEATHER

In Andalusia the temperate Mediterranean climate predominates, characterized by mild winters with irregular rainfall, and dry, hot and sunny summers, which accentuate its characteristics as one moves from the coast to the interior of the region.

It has an average annual temperature of approximately 18ºC, and more than 300 days of sunshine a year, with January being the coldest month and August the hottest.
